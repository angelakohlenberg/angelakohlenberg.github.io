I am a fourth year PhD candidate in Operations Management at the Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University. I am advised by Professor Itai Gurvich.

My research focuses on understanding how impatience impacts the performance and control of dynamic matching markets. This work is motivated by practical applications including organ exchange/allocation, ride-sharing, and perishable inventory allocation (e.g. blood banks and food banks).
